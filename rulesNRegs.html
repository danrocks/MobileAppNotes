There is degree of blur over what is a medical device and what is a "clinical decision support tool". MyVirtualCLinic is a recording device, so no need a CE mark as per the Medical Device Directives.

There are three Medical Device Directives. These have Annexes, some of which are particularly relevant. Some people have put up blogs with further guidance: .
Annoyingly, these seem to be replaced by a Medical Device Regulation (MDR) in 2016

Companys such as Conformance seem to make a business fro CE compliance:

Key directives are:

  • Directive 90/385/EEC regarding active implantable medical devices (AIMD)
  • Directive 93/42/EEC regarding medical devices (MDD)
  • Directive 98/79/EC regarding in vitro diagnostic medical devices (IVDD)

MDD is the main one. MRHA guidance indicates some annexes are particularly key:

  • Annex IX - Classification
  • Annex I - essential requirements
  • Annex X - demonstration of conformity with the essential requirements must include a clinical evaluation.

There is also "relevant harmonised standards" which may be used to demonstrate how many of the requirements have been met.

The EC does provide some further guidance around medical devices and CE marks:

In the Uk the MHRO is the Competent Authority. This web-site only uses hardware in it's examples. However it does link through to the European Commission's MEDDEV site where "MEDDEV 2.1/6 Qualification of and Classification of stand alone software" is worth a read. It gives extensive examples but doesn't directly address basic imaging like mine. If it's just recording information its not a medical device. But if used for monitoring, it is.

Steps to follow to get a CE mark would be:

  1. Complete Technical File - dossier with information about product and providing proof of compliance with applicable requirements
  2. I have an EU office, so don't need an authorised representative
  3. All Class 1 devices must be registered. I think this should be with the Competent Authority. Each EU country sets up it's own Competent Authority to comply with directive. The oversee enforcement of rules
  4. Finalise Declaration of Conformity
